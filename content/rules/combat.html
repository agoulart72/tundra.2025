Rounds & Initiative

Round

A round is about 5-10 seconds. The point in initative order at which you can act is called your turn.

Player Initiative

At the start of combat, players roll a single die each to determine the player order, ties are decided by the players.

GM Initiative

Each enemy has a static default initiative score listed in it's stat block. The GM rolls a number of dice equal to the number of players plus one and pick the highest value, if this beats all of the player rolls, then GM goes first.

Turn Order

All characters and enemies are placed on a single turn track in descending order of their initiative (from highest to lowest, alternating between player and GM). Any remaining enemies act at the end of the round.

Actions

Characters get a total of 3 actions per round. You cannot use the same action more than once on your turn, unless you have a talent that allows so.
All actions are refreshed at the start of the round.

Out of Turn Actions

A player can attempt to take a single action out of it's turn. To do this, you must roll an Awareness check. If the check succeeds you can execute your action before the action that you are interrupting (this action still count as one of your 3 actions). If you fail the check you get a point of Stress. You can attempt this action only once per round.

If the player wants, he can delay one of his actions to happen after his own turn.
At any time before the end of the round, the player can interrupt to use his delayed action. A delayed action happens after the current character or enemy turn.

Zone & Range

A conflict is typically played out using a map.
The map is divided into zones. A zone can be a room, a corridor, or an area of ground. Ths size of the zone can varies depending on the environment, but is usually no larger than 25 meters across.

Zones can have features, which may affects the actions performed within them.
Examples:

  • Cluttered: roll for Mobility when move into the zone. Failure means you fall.
  • Dimly lit: Perception and Ranged attack rolls get a -2 modifier.
  • Cramped: The character can only crawl and its impossible to use Heavy weapons like axes.

Range

The distance between your character and an enemy is divided into five range categories.
  • Engaged : The enemy is right next to the character.
  • Short/Near: A few meters away, in the same zone.
  • Medium: In an adjacent zone, up to 25 meters away.
  • Long: Up to one hundred meters, or four zones away.
  • Distant: As far as the eye can see.

Movement

Movement costs 1 action.
A character can move from one zone to another adjacent zone.
If the character has one or more enemies engaged, you must roll for Mobility, you need one success for each enemy engaged with you. If you fail, you must spend an extra action to move.
During your movement you can attempt to jump (roll Athletics), or open an unlocked door.

Melee / Ranged Combat

To attack in Melee the character must be engaged with the enemy. Some weapons allow the character to attack near enemies in Melee.
When you attack in Melee, you roll the Melee skill plus the weapon bonus.
If the attack succeeds, it causes damage equal to the number of successes plus the weapon base damage.
Enemies defenses are pre-calculated, and they can reduce the amount of damage caused.

Enemies have a pre-calculated damage on attacks.
When an enemy attacks a character, he can use a reaction to defend himself.
A character has a number of reactions per round equal to his Agility Score -1.
Reaction is an active defense, you can roll a defensive skill check, and the number of success reduces the damage the character would take.

  • Mobility - agility based skill to dodge attacks.
  • Melee - if you have a shield or a parrying weapon, you can use Melee to try to block the incoming attack.
If the character has no reactions left when attacked, his only defense is his armour.

To attack with a Ranged attack, if the character is engaged with the enemy, he gets a -3 penalty.
If the enemy is in short or long range, some weapons cause a -1 or -2 modifier to the attack.
Distant enemies have a -3 penalty to attack, and the character must spend an extra ation to Aim.
You roll a Marksmanship skill plus the weapon bonus to attack with a ranged attack.

Weapons

  • Grip - Indicates if the character needs one or two hands to wield the weapon.
  • Bonus - Indicates if the weapon grants any bonus to the attack.
  • Damage - Indicates the base damage of the weapon. This is the damage caused if the attack succeeds.
  • Range - For ranged weapons indicates the penalties for short and long range.
  • Weight - How many regular items the weapon counts as for encumbrance.
  • Features - Any special properties, including the type of damage the weapon causes (B - Bludgeon, P - Piercing, S - Slashing)

Health & Damage

Stamina

Stamina represents how much the character can endure in combat. Stamina is defined by the character's class, plus his BODY score.
When an enemy attacks and hits the character, reduces his Stamina by the amount of damage caused, after reducing the damage by his defense and armor.
If the character's Stamina reaches 0, any futher damage causes 1 Wound.
Stamina is usually recovered by resting or healing, but some talents can also recover Stamina.

Wounds

Wounds represents physical trauma suffered in combat, they are more dangerous than the small cuts and bruises represented by losing Stamina.
For each Wound, you suffer a penalty of -1 to all of your dice rolls, including pushes. Wounds require longer periods of resting or magical healing to be restored.
